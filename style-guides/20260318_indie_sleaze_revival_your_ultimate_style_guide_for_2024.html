Indie Sleaze Revival:
How to Wear the Messiest-Chic Trend of 2024

TREND GUIDE  ·  5 MIN READ  ·  UPDATED JUNE 2025

Okay bestie, let's talk about the trend that's absolutely eating up your FYP right now. The indie sleaze revival is giving 2007-2012 energy — think Alexa Chung at a house party, Skins UK, and American Apparel ads rolled into one chaotic, glamorous moment. TikTok creators have racked up over 800M views on #indiesleaze, and Instagram fits pages are drowning in smudged liner and vintage band tees. It's messy. It's hot. And honestly? It's SO back.

If you've been stuck in your clean-girl era and need fresh outfit ideas, this is your sign to let loose. Here's your no-gatekeep style guide to nailing indie sleaze without looking like you're wearing a costume.

1. Lead with a Thrashed Band Tee (or Vintage Baby Tee)

This is your anchor piece. Grab a slightly oversized graphic tee — Nirvana, The Strokes, Joy Division, whatever speaks to your soul — and style it tucked loosely into low-rise anything. A shrunken baby tee with a faded print works even harder. The key? It should look like you've owned it forever. Thrift stores and vintage resellers are your best friend here. This single piece is how to wear indie sleaze in its simplest form.

2. Low-Rise Is Non-Negotiable

I know, I know — we just recovered from the low-rise discourse. But the indie sleaze revival literally runs on hip-grazing waistlines. We're talking faded skinny jeans, micro denim skirts, or even a beat-up pair of American Apparel-style disco pants if you're feeling unhinged. Pair with a studded belt that sits loosely on your hips and you're already 80% there. No cap, this silhouette is the backbone of every iconic indie sleaze outfit idea.

3. Layer a Worn-In Leather Jacket Over Everything

A slightly distressed leather moto jacket is the ultimate indie sleaze uniform piece. Throw it over a slip dress, a band tee, a going-out top — literally anything. The more beat-up it looks, the better. Faux leather works perfectly and is way more budget-friendly. Pro tip: size up one for that effortless, borrowed-from-your-cool-ex vibe.

4. Accessories = Controlled Chaos

This is where you go feral (affectionate). Stack thin gold and silver necklaces together — mixing metals is very indie sleaze-coded. Add wayfarers, a cross-body vintage bag, and chunky rings. Smudged black eyeliner and undone hair aren't technically accessories but they ARE the vibe. The whole point is looking like you rolled out of a really good night and still look incredible. That's the entire style guide energy.
