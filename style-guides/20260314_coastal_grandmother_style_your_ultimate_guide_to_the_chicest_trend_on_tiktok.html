Coastal Grandmother Style: Your Ultimate Guide to the Chicest Trend on TikTok

Updated June 2024 · 4 min read

Okay, let's talk about it — coastal grandmother style is absolutely everywhere on TikTok and Instagram, and honestly? It's giving timeless, unbothered elegance and we are HERE for it. Think Nancy Meyers movies, Diane Keaton in Something's Gotta Give, farmers market mornings, and that effortless "I summer in the Hamptons" energy. Whether you're 22 or 35, this aesthetic is the perfect antidote to loud, maximalist fashion fatigue. It's soft, it's clean, and it's *chef's kiss*.

If your FYP has been screaming "linen pants" and "how to wear neutral tones," you're not alone. This style guide breaks down exactly how to nail the look without looking like you raided your grandma's actual closet (no shade to grandma though — she understood the assignment).

1. Linen Is Your Entire Personality Now

No literally — linen everything. Wide-leg linen pants, oversized linen button-downs, linen shorts. The slightly wrinkled, lived-in texture IS the vibe. Stick to cream, oatmeal, soft white, and light khaki. Pro tip: size up one for that relaxed, breezy silhouette. This is the cornerstone of any coastal grandmother outfit ideas list, and the easiest entry point into the trend.

2. Layer Like You're Expecting a Sea Breeze

A cable-knit sweater draped over your shoulders? Iconic behavior. A lightweight cotton cardigan over a simple tank? *Perfection.* Coastal grandmother style is all about soft, casual layering — nothing structured, nothing try-hard. Think cozy not bulky. A cashmere-blend crewneck in ecru or soft blue is genuinely the hardest-working piece in this whole aesthetic. Throw it over literally anything and you're styled.

3. Accessories Stay Quiet (But Expensive-Looking)

Put the chunky chains away bestie — this look calls for understated gold jewelry, a classic straw tote, tortoiseshell sunglasses, and maybe a silk scarf if you're feeling fancy. The whole how to wear this trend correctly comes down to restraint. One or two quiet-luxury accessories elevate the entire outfit. A woven leather belt or simple canvas espadrilles complete the look without trying too hard.

4. The Color Palette Is Non-Negotiable

We're talking: ivory, sand, chambray blue, soft sage, warm taupe, and classic navy. That's it. That's the palette. If your outfit could blend into a beach house interior, you've nailed it. Avoid harsh blacks, neons, or loud prints. This is the aesthetic equivalent of a deep breath — calm, collected, curated.

So whether you're headed to brunch, a bookstore, or the actual coast — consider this your sign to lean all the way into the vibes.
