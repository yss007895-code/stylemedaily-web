Coastal Grandmother Style: Your Ultimate Guide to Nailing the Trend

STYLE GUIDE · 5 MIN READ

Okay bestie, let's talk about the aesthetic that has absolutely taken over TikTok and Instagram — coastal grandmother style. Think Nancy Meyers movies, Diane Keaton in Something's Gotta Give, farmers market runs with a wicker basket, and reading on a breezy porch with a glass of white wine. It's giving effortless. It's giving old money. It's giving "I summer in the Hamptons but make it chill."

Whether you live near the coast or are fully landlocked, this style guide is your roadmap to channeling that relaxed, refined energy. The best part? You don't need a trust fund — just a few key pieces and the right vibe. Here are your outfit ideas and tips to absolutely slay this look.

1. Linen Is Your Entire Personality Now

No seriously — linen is the backbone of coastal grandmother style. We're talking wide-leg linen pants, oversized linen button-downs, and flowy linen dresses in soft neutrals like oatmeal, white, chambray blue, and sand. The wrinkles? They're a feature, not a bug. It screams "I woke up this gorgeous" without trying too hard. Layer a linen shirt open over a simple white tank and you've already got the blueprint for how to wear this trend effortlessly.

2. Stick to a Soft, Neutral Color Palette

The coastal grandma color palette is *chef's kiss*. Think whites, creams, taupes, soft blues, sage greens, and the occasional navy stripe. No neon. No chaos. Just tonal, sophisticated calm. The key to making your outfit ideas feel elevated is keeping everything in the same tonal family — monochromatic fits are literally undefeated with this aesthetic. Pro tip: a cream-on-cream look with gold jewelry? Obsessed.

3. Accessories That Say "I Have a Beach House"

Accessories make or break this vibe. You need: a woven tote or straw bag, classic gold hoops, a silk scarf (tied loosely, obviously), and a good pair of leather sandals or white sneakers. Sunglasses are non-negotiable — go for oversized tortoiseshell or classic aviators. A cotton bucket hat or a wide-brim sun hat is the ultimate finishing touch. These are the details that take your coastal grandmother style from "nice outfit" to "she gets it."

4. Invest in One Perfect Knit

Every coastal grandma needs a casually draped sweater — preferably cable-knit, preferably cream or light blue. Throw it over your shoulders like you're about to take a sunset stroll and it's getting "a little breezy, darling." A lightweight cotton or cashmere crewneck is the perfect transitional piece for how to wear this trend from spring through fall. Pair it with tailored trousers or straight-leg jeans and you're giving main character energy.
