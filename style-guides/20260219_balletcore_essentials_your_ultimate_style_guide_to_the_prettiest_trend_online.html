1. The Wrap Top Is Your New Best Friend

A ballet wrap top or cardigan is literally the cornerstone piece. Layer it over a fitted cami or a simple bodysuit and pair with high-waisted trousers or a midi skirt. The crossover silhouette is insanely flattering on every body type, and it instantly reads "I have taste." Pro tip: go for soft knits in blush, cream, or pale lilac. This single swap elevates your outfit ideas from basic to *chef's kiss*.

2. Lean Into the Soft Color Palette

Balletcore lives in the muted, romantic spectrum ‚Äî think dusty rose, ivory, dove grey, and barely-there lavender. The vibe is tonal dressing, so don't be afraid to go monochromatic. A head-to-toe blush moment? Absolutely iconic. If all-pink feels like a lot, ground it with a warm taupe or oatmeal layer. The key to making this how to wear approach work is keeping textures interesting: mix satin with ribbed knit, or mesh with cotton.

3. Ballet Flats > Everything

The flat girlies have WON. Ballet flats (especially ones with ribbon ties or a cute bow detail) are the shoe of the moment and honestly the comfiest way to participate in this trend. They go with literally everything ‚Äî wide-leg pants, flowy skirts, even your favorite relaxed jeans. This is the easiest entry point for anyone just dipping their toes into balletcore essentials. No stilettos required.

4. Accessorize With Ribbons & Dainty Jewelry

This is where you take the look from "cute outfit" to full *aesthetic*. Satin hair ribbons, pearl studs, delicate chain necklaces, and thin bangles all channel that effortless ballerina elegance.
