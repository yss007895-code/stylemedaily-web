Balletcore Aesthetic 2025

Graceful, Feminine, and Effortlessly Elegant

Why Balletcore Endures

Balletcore first swept social media in 2022, but by 2025 it has matured into a fully realized aesthetic movement. Inspired by the ballet studio — not the stage — this trend celebrates soft fabrics, delicate details, and the quiet discipline of classical dance translated into everyday fashion.

Miu Miu, Sandy Liang, and Simone Rocha have all incorporated ballet references into their recent collections, proving this trend has serious fashion credibility.

Key Pieces to Own

Ribbon Ballet Flats

The signature balletcore shoe. Ankle-tie ribbons in satin or grosgrain elevate any outfit with feminine charm.

Wrap Top or Cardigan

Inspired by ballet warm-up layers. A crossover knit in blush, cream, or black is endlessly versatile.

Tulle Midi Skirt

Romantic and floaty, a tulle skirt brings instant ballerina energy. Pair with a simple bodysuit for balance.

Fitted Bodysuit

The modern leotard. Choose a scooped or boat neck in neutral tones as your base layer.

Styling Tips

Pro Tip: The key to balletcore is restraint. Keep your color palette soft (blush, ivory, lavender, pale grey) and let one statement piece — like ribbon flats or a tulle skirt — be the focal point.

For work, pair a wrap cardigan with tailored trousers and ballet flats. Add pearl studs for a polished finish that still feels soft and approachable.

For a weekend brunch look, try a fitted bodysuit tucked into a tulle midi skirt with a cropped denim jacket thrown over. Ribbon flats and a mini shoulder bag complete the ensemble.

Color Palette

Soft and muted: blush pink, powder blue, ivory, lavender, ballet slipper nude, and dove grey. Black works as an accent, especially in bodysuits and wraps, echoing the classic rehearsal studio aesthetic.
