Balletcore Essentials: Your Ultimate Style Guide to the Prettiest Trend on TikTok

TREND GUIDE  ·  6 MIN READ

Okay bestie, let's talk about the aesthetic that has absolutely taken over our FYP and Explore pages — balletcore. Think soft pinks, ribbon details, wrap tops, and that effortlessly graceful energy that makes you feel like you're starring in your own Black Swan era (minus the drama). This trend isn't just viral — it's a whole mood shift toward feminine, elegant dressing that honestly feels like a warm hug for your wardrobe.

Whether you're a dancer or have never done a plié in your life, these balletcore essentials are incredibly wearable. Below, I'm breaking down exactly how to wear the trend IRL with outfit ideas that are giving main character energy — no tutu required. Consider this your official style guide. 🩰

1. Start With the Wrap Top — It's Non-Negotiable

The ballet wrap top is literally the hero piece of this entire aesthetic. A soft knit wrap in blush, cream, or black instantly gives you that dancer-off-duty look. Pair it with high-waisted jeans for a casual outfit idea, or tuck it into a midi skirt for full balletcore immersion. The crossover silhouette is insanely flattering on every body type — it cinches at the smallest part of your waist and the vibe is *chef's kiss*. Pro tip: look for ones with ribbon ties for that extra delicate detail.

2. Ballet Flats Are THE Shoe (Yes, Again)

Ballet flats had their first moment in the early 2000s and now they're back with a vengeance — except this time we're doing them right. Think Mary Janes with satin finishes, cap-toe flats in nude or burgundy, and lace-up styles that literally wrap around your ankle like actual pointe shoe ribbons. They go with everything: wide-leg trousers, slip dresses, even your fave baggy jeans. This is the ultimate quiet luxury shoe that says "I have taste" without screaming. If you're building your balletcore essentials wardrobe, start here.

3. Layer With Sheer & Tulle (But Make It Wearable)

Tulle skirts and sheer fabrics might sound costume-y, but the key to nailing this style guide tip is subtlety. A sheer mesh long-sleeve layered under a tank top adds that ethereal texture without going full recital. A tulle midi skirt in a muted tone — think dusty rose, dove grey, or champagne — paired with a fitted tee and sneakers keeps things street-style cool. The contrast between soft and structured is what makes these outfit ideas actually feel modern and not like you raided a costume closet.

4. Accessories: Ribbons, Bows & Dainty Everything

The finishing touches are where balletcore really comes alive. Hair ribbons and satin bows are having a massive moment — tie one around a low bun or a slicked-back ponytail and you're instantly Pinterest-worthy. Layer dainty gold or pearl jewelry, grab a small structured bag in a neutral tone, and add leg warmers over your flats if you're feeling bold. These little details are how to wear the trend in a way that feels intentional, elevated, and totally you.
