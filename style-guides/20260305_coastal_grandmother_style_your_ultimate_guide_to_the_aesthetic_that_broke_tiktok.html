Coastal Grandmother Style: Your Ultimate Guide to the Aesthetic That Broke TikTok

Style Guide · 5 min read

Okay bestie, let's talk about coastal grandmother style — the aesthetic that absolutely took over TikTok and Instagram and shows zero signs of slowing down. Think Nancy Meyers movies, Diane Keaton in Something's Gotta Give, breezy linen everything, and the energy of someone who owns beachfront property and a really good olive oil collection. It's giving effortless, it's giving old money quiet luxury, and honestly? It's the most wearable trend we've seen in years. Whether you're actually near the coast or manifesting it from your apartment, this style guide has all the outfit ideas you need to nail the look.

1. Linen Is Your Entire Personality Now

No notes — linen is the backbone of this whole vibe. A relaxed-fit linen button-down in white, oatmeal, or soft blue is literally the how to wear cheat code for coastal grandmother. Pair it with wide-leg linen trousers or throw it over a simple tank and shorts. The wrinkles? They're a feature, not a bug. Roll the sleeves, leave a few buttons undone, and let the fabric do its thing. Effortless elegance unlocked.

2. Neutrals Only (With One Exception)

Your color palette is cream, white, khaki, soft camel, chambray blue, and sage green. That's it. That's the tweet. The one exception? Navy stripes — very Breton, very chic, very "I summer in the Hamptons." These outfit ideas practically style themselves when your whole wardrobe is in the same tonal family. No color-matching anxiety, just vibes.

3. Accessories That Scream "I Have a Garden"

This is where you really sell the fantasy. A woven straw tote, oversized sunglasses (the bigger the better), delicate gold jewelry, and a wide-brim sun hat are non-negotiable. Footwear? Think leather sandals, classic white sneakers, or simple loafers. The goal is looking like you just came from a farmer's market where you bought heirloom tomatoes and fresh lavender. It's an energy.

4. Layer Like You Live Somewhere With a Sea Breeze

How to wear this trend year-round? Layering is the move. A lightweight cable-knit sweater draped over your shoulders (NOT through the sleeves — this is crucial), a cotton cardigan over a white tee, or a trench coat for cooler days. It's the polished-but-relaxed layering that makes coastal grandmother style slay in any season. Very main character doing a morning walk on the beach energy.
