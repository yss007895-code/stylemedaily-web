Coastal Grandmother Style: Your Ultimate Guide to the Chicest Trend on TikTok

STYLE GUIDE • 6 MIN READ

Okay, let's talk about the coastal grandmother style aesthetic — because if your FYP hasn't been flooded with linen pants, wicker baskets, and Nancy Meyers movie energy, where have you even been? 🐚 This trend is giving main character energy meets your most elegant auntie who summers in the Hamptons. Think Diane Keaton in Something's Gotta Give, but make it Gen Z. It's soft, it's timeless, and honestly? It's the most effortlessly chic outfit idea to dominate your feed right now.

The best part? You don't need a beach house to pull this off. Here's your no-fluff style guide on how to wear coastal grandmother like a pro.

1. Linen Is Your New Best Friend

This whole aesthetic literally runs on linen. Oversized linen shirts, wide-leg linen trousers, flowy linen dresses — all of it. Stick to a neutral palette: think oat milk, soft white, sand, and pale blue. The vibe is "I just came from a farmer's market with fresh flowers" and honestly, we're here for it. Layer a relaxed linen button-down over a simple tank and you've got an outfit idea that's both low-effort and impossibly put-together.

2. Quiet Luxury Accessories Only

Less is more, bestie. We're talking slim gold jewelry, tortoiseshell sunglasses, a classic straw tote, and maybe a silk scarf tied loosely around your neck or bag. No loud logos. No chunky statement pieces. The coastal grandmother style is all about understated elegance — the kind of accessories that whisper "old money" without trying. A good pair of leather sandals or classic white sneakers? *Chef's kiss.*

3. Master the Effortless Layering

Here's how to wear this trend year-round: layering. A cream cable-knit sweater draped over your shoulders (not actually worn — this is key), a lightweight cashmere cardigan over a white tee, or a cotton blazer with rolled sleeves. It looks like you didn't try but also like you definitely have a subscription to Architectural Digest. That's the sweet spot.

4. Keep Your Color Story Clean

This is non-negotiable for the aesthetic. Your entire palette should feel like a beach at golden hour: ivory, taupe, chambray blue, sage green, warm white, and khaki. Monochromatic fits especially go hard here — an all-cream outfit with tan accessories is genuinely undefeated. If you want a pop of color, keep it muted: dusty rose or soft seafoam. That's it. That's the whole style guide.
