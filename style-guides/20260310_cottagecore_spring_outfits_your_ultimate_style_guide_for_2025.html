Cottagecore Spring Outfits That Are Taking Over Your FYP

STYLE GUIDE · SPRING 2025

Okay bestie, let's talk about the aesthetic that absolutely refuses to leave our feeds — and honestly? We're not mad about it. Cottagecore spring outfits are everywhere on TikTok and Instagram right now, and this year's version feels less "Little House on the Prairie" cosplay and more effortlessly romantic main character energy. Think linen, wildflowers, soft ruffles, and that golden-hour glow. If your Pinterest board has been screaming pastoral fantasy lately, this style guide is your sign to finally commit. 🌿

1. Lead With Linen — It's Giving Main Character

The easiest outfit idea for nailing cottagecore this spring? A linen midi dress in a muted neutral — oat, sage, butter, or soft blush. Linen literally breathes spring. Pair it with woven flats or leather sandals and you're done. No overthinking required. The slightly rumpled texture is the whole vibe, so don't stress about wrinkles — that's the aesthetic, babe. Layer a cropped cardigan over it for chilly mornings and you've got a fit that works from farmers market to rooftop brunch.

2. Floral Prints, But Make Them Vintage

Here's how to wear florals without looking like your grandma's couch (respectfully): go ditsy or botanical. Tiny scattered prints on a puff-sleeve blouse tucked into high-waisted wide-leg trousers? Obsessed. The trick is keeping the color palette muted — think dusty rose, lavender, and cream rather than neon garden vibes. A ribbon or silk scarf tied loosely in your hair takes this from cute to cottagecore perfection. TikTok girlies have been eating this up, and the engagement is unreal.

3. The "Soft Girl Meets Country" Accessory Stack

Accessories are where your cottagecore spring outfit goes from basic to *chef's kiss*. We're talking straw tote bags, delicate gold layered necklaces, pearl studs, and — hear me out — a woven sun hat. Not the floppy beach kind; a structured, slightly smaller brim that says "I forage for wildflowers and also have impeccable taste." Basket bags are having a MOMENT and honestly they hold everything. Function meets fairy tale.

4. Ground It With the Right Shoes

The shoe game matters more than you think. Ballet flats, mary janes, or leather ankle boots (in cognac or cream) keep the look polished without trying too hard. Avoid anything too chunky or streetwear-coded — the goal is softness. If you really want to commit, a pair of ribbon-tie espadrilles will have people stopping you to ask for your style guide. Pro tip: match your shoe tone to your bag for that quiet-luxury cohesion that makes a fit look expensive on zero effort.
