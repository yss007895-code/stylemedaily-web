Eclectic Grandpa Style Is Taking Over — Here's How to Wear It

Trend Guide • 2025

Oversized · Vintage · Intentional

Okay bestie, let's talk about the aesthetic that has absolutely devoured my TikTok FYP and Instagram Explore page: eclectic grandpa style. Think oversized knits, vintage tweeds, loafers that look borrowed from your cool grandpa's closet, and layering that somehow feels both chaotic and genius. It's giving cozy intellectual meets thrift-store treasure hunter, and honestly? It's the most personality-packed trend we've seen in a minute.

This vibe blew up because it's the ultimate "no rules" approach to fashion. It's anti-clean-girl, anti-minimalist — it's maximalist comfort with a side of "I just know things." Whether you're channeling Diane Keaton in Annie Hall or literally raiding your grandpa's attic, this style guide has the outfit ideas you need to absolutely nail the look. Let's get into it. 👇

1. Oversized Is Non-Negotiable

The foundation of eclectic grandpa style? Nothing should fit "perfectly." We're talking chunky cable-knit sweaters that swallow you whole, wide-leg trousers with a relaxed pleat, and blazers with shoulders that extend past yours. The key to making oversized look intentional (not sloppy) is picking one structured piece — like a tailored vest or a leather belt cinching a cardigan. That contrast is what makes the outfit read "fashion" instead of "laundry day." This is literally the easiest outfit idea to start with if you're new to the trend.

2. Mix Patterns Like You DGAF (But You Totally Do)

Argyle vest over a striped button-down? Plaid trousers with a Fair Isle knit? YES and yes. The "eclectic" part of eclectic grandpa style lives in the pattern clash. The trick that separates chaotic from chic: keep your patterns in the same color family. Earth tones are your best friend here — think rust, olive, cream, burgundy, and camel. When the palette is cohesive, you can go absolutely unhinged with the prints and it'll still look sickening. This is the how to wear tip that'll change your whole approach to getting dressed.

3. Accessories = The Main Character Energy

No eclectic grandpa fit is complete without the accessories. We're talking round tortoiseshell glasses (prescription or not, no judgment), a worn leather crossbody bag, a newsboy cap, or a vintage-looking watch. Layered gold and silver together? Absolutely — grandpa doesn't follow jewelry rules and neither do you. Loafers or chunky Mary Janes on the feet seal the deal. These small details are what make people stop scrolling and hit save on your OOTD post.

4. Thrift First, Buy Second

Real talk: this aesthetic was built for thrifting. The slightly worn-in quality of secondhand pieces adds authenticity you literally cannot buy new. Hit up your local Goodwill, Depop, or your actual grandparent's closet before shopping retail. Look for corduroy blazers, knit vests, wool trousers, and leather belts with patina. The beauty of this style guide approach is that imperfection IS the look. A small moth hole? That's ✨character✨.
